Lessons from the Himalayas

In the remote highlands where my family lives, surrounded by vast landscapes and harsh conditions, I learned more about happiness, community, and resilience than any classroom could teach.

Lesson 1: Less can be more

My cousins in India had fewer material possessions than I did, yet they seemed happier. Their joy came from relationships, from purpose, from connection to something larger than themselves.

Lesson 2: Community is everything

In nomadic culture, survival depends on cooperation. Everyone looks out for each other. There's no room for rugged individualism when you're living in the highlands – and that interdependence creates bonds that modern society often lacks.

Lesson 3: Nature is not separate from us

Living close to the land isn't just about sustainability – it's about understanding our place in the world. When you depend on nature for everything, you develop a respect and connection that transforms how you see yourself.

Lesson 4: Purpose comes from service

Whether it was teaching English to eager students or helping my cousin learn the dranyen, I found that the moments when I felt most alive were when I was helping others grow.

These lessons aren't just nice ideas – they're practical wisdom that we desperately need in our modern world of anxiety, disconnection, and climate crisis.
